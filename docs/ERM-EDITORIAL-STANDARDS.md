## 1. Core Principle

ERM Advisory does not publish content designed to chase algorithms. ERM Advisory publishes **operator-grade content designed to help executives make better decisions.**

Every article, framework, scorecard, and resource must prioritize:

1. Credibility
2. Practicality
3. Original thinking
4. Revenue relevance
5. Long-term value

over:

- Clickbait
- Trend chasing
- Unsupported claims
- Generic AI commentary
- Marketing hype

---

## 2. Content Quality Standard

Before publishing any asset, ask one question:

> Would a CMO, CEO, CRO, GTM leader, or board member learn something useful from this?

If the answer is no, rewrite it. Depth over volume. One useful idea, fully developed, beats ten shallow ones.

---

## 3. Original IP Standard

Every major article should introduce at least one of the following:

- New framework
- New operating model
- New assessment
- New scorecard
- New metric
- New terminology
- New visual model

**Reference IP already in the library:** Marketing Execution Gap, Revenue Execution Gap, Agentic Execution Gap, AI Buying Committee, Signal-Centric ABM, Buying Group Mapping, Buying Group Orchestration, Account Activation Gap, Demand Architecture, the 4D ICP Framework, the AI Visibility Architecture, the AI Visibility Gap, and Share of Model.

ERM content should **create** intellectual property, not summarize what others already wrote.

---

## 4. Executive Language Standard

Write like an operator, not a vendor.

**Avoid (banned):** revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, cutting-edge, best-in-class, world-class, transformative, and similar hype.

**Prefer:** operational, strategic, measurable, practical, observable.

Avoid superlatives that cannot be defended. State what is true, specifically, and let the reader draw the conclusion.

---

## 5. Statistics & Research Standard

**No statistic may appear without a source.**

Every claim involving percentages, adoption rates, market forecasts, buyer behavior, benchmarks, or survey findings must include:

- The original source
- A hyperlink
- The publication year

If no credible source exists, **remove the statistic and replace it with an observation.**

> BAD: "Nine in ten buyers use AI."
> GOOD: "Buyers increasingly use AI systems during vendor evaluation."

Never launder a secondary aggregator's number as if it were primary research. Cite the primary source or do not cite a number at all.

---

## 6. Source & Citation Standard

Every article, framework, scorecard, and PDF must include a **Research & Supporting Evidence** section.

- **Minimum:** 3 authoritative sources
- **Preferred:** 5 or more

**Approved sources:** Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey, Bain, BCG, Deloitte, PwC, EY, Accenture, Microsoft, Google, LinkedIn, Salesforce, Adobe, IBM, IDC, Omdia, HBR.

All sources must be hyperlinked, with the publication year stated. No unsupported statistics.

---

## 7. Timelessness Standard

Prioritize evergreen insight. Avoid content that becomes outdated in six months.

Focus on durable subjects: buying behavior, organizational behavior, revenue operations, decision-making, execution systems, GTM strategy, and marketing operations.

Technology changes. Operating principles last. Name specific tools and vendors only as **examples**, never as the load-bearing idea. A framework must remain valid if the current vendor names are replaced.

---

## 8. Framework Standard

Every framework must include:

- Problem definition
- Framework diagram
- Strategic explanation
- Measurement approach
- Practical application
- FAQ
- Related resources
- Schema markup
- Research & Supporting Evidence

A framework must be able to **stand alone** without requiring the associated blog article.

---

## 9. Article Standard

Every article must include:

- Executive Summary
- Featured Snippet Answer
- Original framework or model
- Assessment section
- Action plan
- FAQ section
- Internal links
- External sources (Research & Supporting Evidence)

Target length: **3,000 to 4,500 words.** Depth over volume.

---

## 10. Accessibility (ADA) Standard

- Plain language where possible
- Short paragraphs (3 to 4 sentences maximum)
- Descriptive headings and clear hierarchy
- Accessible tables (caption, header scope) and real lists
- No walls of text
- AA color contrast for all text
- Accessible visuals (role, title, description on diagrams; meaningful alt text)
- Mobile-friendly formatting

---

## 11. Discoverability (AEO / SEO) Standard

- Lead with a direct, self-contained answer a model can lift verbatim
- Use question-shaped headings that match real buyer queries
- Define every coined term in one sentence
- Include BlogPosting / CreativeWork, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList schema
- Maintain canonical URLs and consistent entity descriptions
- Optimize for traditional search and for AI answer engines (AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), without sacrificing any standard above

Discoverability is a consequence of quality, never a substitute for it.

## 13. Publishing Surfaces & Card Standards (added after the June 2026 publication audit)

**Homepage featured card:** 35 to 60 words, 2 to 3 sentences. Create tension and curiosity. Do not summarize the framework or explain methodology. No awkward wrapping, widows, or orphans.

**Blog archive featured card:** 25 to 45 words, 1 to 2 sentences. Lead with the business problem, the buyer-behavior shift, or competitive tension. No framework explanations, methodology, or AEO jargon in the excerpt.

**Every new article must be wired into:** the homepage featured grid, the blog archive (as featured card if newest), the sitemap, and the relevant framework's "Related Articles." No orphan assets.

## 14. Asset Naming Convention

- **OG and social images:** lowercase, hyphenated, `erm-` prefix, descriptive, ending `-og-image.png` (e.g. `erm-answer-engine-optimization-b2b-og-image.png`, `erm-ai-visibility-architecture-framework-og-image.png`).
- **Framework and resource PDFs:** match the existing library convention, capitalized and hyphenated with `ERM-` prefix (e.g. `ERM-AI-Visibility-Scorecard.pdf`), so the resource library stays internally consistent.
- No generic, duplicate, or temporary filenames.

## 15. Downloadable PDF Requirements

Every downloadable PDF must include: the canonical framework URL, at least one related-framework link, an author link (Erik R. Miller), an ERM Advisory link, and a hyperlinked Research & Supporting Evidence section. All links must be live (embedded with linkURL).
