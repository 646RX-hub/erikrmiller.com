# Strategic Positioning Review — AI Visibility Architecture

**Type:** Thought-leadership positioning review (not design, not SEO).
**Subject:** AI Visibility Architecture framework, scorecard, visuals, AEO article, social, roadmap.
**Goal:** Make AI Visibility Architecture read as a category-defining executive framework, not an article about AEO.
**Prepared by:** Content Strategy Lead, ERM Advisory · June 12, 2026
**Status of edits:** Items marked **[done]** are already implemented on the live assets. Items marked **[recommended]** are queued.

---

## 1. Strategic Critique

The framework is strong on substance and weak on altitude. The IP is real and defensible (four layers, the AI Visibility Gap, Share of Model), but the page led with the **mechanism** (AEO) rather than the **problem** (losing the deal before sales engages). Executives do not convene to improve AEO. They convene because a competitor is being recommended and they are not.

The fix is altitude, not rewriting. Lead with the business outcome, demote AEO to the explanation, and let the proprietary metric carry the executive value. The single most quotable line on the page, *"You can be well known to people and nearly invisible to models,"* is the natural spine of the whole narrative and was previously buried mid-page.

## 2. Messaging Weaknesses

1. **Mechanism-led, not outcome-led.** The hero and section labels (`SEO vs AEO`, `Who Owns AEO`) framed the page around a marketing technique. **[done]** Relabeled to business framing (`The Business Problem`, `From Ranking to Recommendation`, `Who Owns AI Visibility`).
2. **AEO over-indexed.** "AEO" appeared as the headline concept repeatedly. **[done]** AEO is now positioned explicitly as *the mechanism, not the goal.*
3. **The shortlist narrative was implicit.** The central, repeatable truth, *buyers build the shortlist inside AI before they contact sales*, was stated once. **[done]** It now leads the hero and recurs as the spine.
4. **Recommendation under-emphasized.** The page described visibility and citation more than the outcome executives care about: being **recommended** and **selected.** **[done]** "AI Recommendation" is now an explicit stage in copy and diagram.
5. **Share of Model was half-explained.** It was strong enough to be its own asset but was being spent inside this page. **[done]** Trimmed to a teaser plus a forthcoming-framework note.

## 3. Positioning Gaps

- **No ecosystem story.** The three AI frameworks read as isolated assets. **[done]** Added a "Three Frameworks, One System" section naming the **ERM Advisory AI Operating System**: AI Buying Committee (demand) → AI Visibility Architecture (supply) → Share of Model (measurement).
- **Weak knowledge graph.** Few internal citations to sibling frameworks. **[done]** Added contextual links to six frameworks and four articles.
- **Search-flavored visuals.** The comparison graphic said "The Shift to AI Search," which reads as a marketer's concern. **[done]** Changed to "The Shift to AI-Mediated Buying."
- **Outcome not explicit in the diagram.** The diagram implied but did not state the business result. **[done]** Diagram now shows **AI Visibility → AI Recommendation → Share of Model**, with Share of Model rendered as the business metric.

## 6. Recommended Framework Roadmap — The ERM Advisory AI Operating System

```
AI Buying Committee Framework   (Framework #1 in the AI family)  — DEMAND side
        ↓        how buyers delegate research and evaluation to AI
AI Visibility Architecture      (Framework #2 in the AI family)  — SUPPLY side
        ↓        how vendors become visible, citable, and recommendable
Share of Model Framework        (Future Framework #9)            — MEASUREMENT
                 how you measure, report, and attribute AI recommendation
```

This trilogy is the **ERM Advisory AI Operating System**. The framework page now names it explicitly and links the two existing members. Position all three as one system in the hub, the nav, and social.

## 7. Recommended Social Content Strategy

- **Anchor post (quote-led):** lead with *"You can be well known to people and nearly invisible to models."* Single idea, no diagram, maximum quotability. This is the line designed to be screenshotted.
- **Carousel:** the comparison graphic, retitled "The Shift to AI-Mediated Buying," one row per slide, ending on Share of Model.
- **Diagram post:** the new outcome-chain diagram with the caption "Visibility is not the goal. Recommendation is. Here is how to measure it."
- **Executive-question hook posts:** four short posts, each opening with one of the executive questions ("Why is ChatGPT recommending our competitor?") and resolving to the framework.
- **Series teaser:** announce the AI Operating System trilogy and tease the forthcoming Share of Model framework to build anticipation, not to give away the methodology.
- **Cadence discipline:** every post links to the framework page (canonical), never to the blog first. The framework is the entity you want AI to learn.

## 8. Elevating Share of Model into a Standalone Framework

Treat Share of Model as the highest-value emerging IP in the library. Do **not** fully explain it inside AI Visibility Architecture. The current page now teases it and reserves the methodology. The standalone build-out should include:

- **Future Framework #9: Share of Model** — buyer-question selection, citation tracking, recommendation tracking, description-accuracy scoring, reporting cadence, executive dashboards, revenue attribution, and pipeline influence.
- **Future blog series:** "How to Measure Share of Model" (3 to 5 parts), each a citable, single-method article.
- **Future scorecard:** a dedicated Share of Model Assessment, distinct from the AI Visibility Scorecard.
- **Future executive dashboard:** a live artifact or template that reports Share of Model to leadership on a cadence.

Sequencing: ship the framework page first, then the dashboard template, then the blog series. The metric becomes the recurring measurement standard that the other two frameworks feed into, which is what makes the trilogy an operating system rather than three essays.
